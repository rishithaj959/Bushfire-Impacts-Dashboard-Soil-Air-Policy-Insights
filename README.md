# Bushfire-Impacts-Dashboard-Soil-Air-Policy-Insights
The Bushfire Impacts Dashboard is an interactive R Shiny tool that combines maps, time-series data, 3D visualizations, and predictive models to show how bushfires impact soil nutrients, air quality, and future environmental risk across Australia. Users explore fire severity, nutrient loss, and pollution trends through filters and themed tabs.

Bushfires continue to be one of the most critical environmental challenges facing Australia, exacerbated by climate change, prolonged droughts, and increasingly unpredictable weather patterns. These events not only devastate native vegetation and biodiversity but also have far-reaching impacts on soil quality, air pollution levels, and community health. As the frequency and intensity of bushfires rise, it becomes increasingly important to understand their cascading effects across ecological and human systems in a comprehensive, data-driven manner.
This project introduces the Bushfire Impacts Dashboard—an interactive narrative visualisation platform that unifies geospatial incident data, laboratory soil chemistry results, air quality readings, and predictive modeling outputs. By weaving together static and temporal datasets into a multi-tabbed narrative, the dashboard provides an integrated exploration of bushfire intensity, soil nutrient depletion, pollutant dynamics, and long-term forecasted risks. Each section of the dashboard presents a targeted storyline, using visual metaphors such as maps, streamgraphs, parallel coordinates, and 3D surface plots to guide users through interconnected patterns and insights.
The visualisation aims to support four key themes:
(1) the spatial spread and intensity of fires,
(2) nutrient leaching and soil degradation,
(3) pollutant spikes and air quality deterioration, and
(4) long-term environmental forecasting.
Through user-driven controls like sliders, dropdown filters, and animated plots, viewers can tailor the insights to their region of interest, pollutant type, or temperature band—enhancing both engagement and analytical depth.
The primary audience for this visualisation includes a wide range of stakeholders such as:
•	Environmental policy makers and land management agencies seeking data to guide resource allocation, restoration planning, and preventive strategies.
•	Public health officials and regulators interested in pollutant risk detection and air quality monitoring.
•	Academic researchers and data scientists looking for structured data insights and visual tools to support environmental modeling and climate adaptation studies.
Ultimately, this dashboard transforms complex multi-dimensional datasets into a cohesive and interactive narrative. By doing so, it empowers informed decision-making, strengthens early warning capabilities, and fosters cross-sector collaboration for climate resilience and ecological recovery.
